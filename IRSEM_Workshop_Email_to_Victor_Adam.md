## The Format Reality Check

Just wrapped up the Fletcher workshop here in Boston - we had **3 days × 3 hours** (9 hours total), which gave us tons of breathing room for interactive exercises. Participants could really dig into the tools, make mistakes, iterate, and genuinely learn by doing.

For IRSEM, we're looking at **4 hours total** - less than half the time. This fundamentally changes the pedagogical equation.

## The Russian Military Angle

Here's the interesting twist though: IRSEM specifically wants to see our Russian military research, which honestly wasn't the main focus at Fletcher (they were more interested in the general methodology).

We've got 3+ years of pretty unique stuff to showcase:
- The WACKO framework (which no one else is doing)
- Our regeneration capabilities assessments (quantitative, not just hand-waving)
- The red lines analysis (50,000+ statements processed)
- RFSDP work (the full corpus → sunburst → ExSum pipeline)
- The GNN/causal modeling (still early but compelling)
- All the Duma/FC/Telegram systematic analysis

This is research that IRSEM can't get anywhere else - they're doing traditional strategic analysis while we're doing computational intelligence at scale.

## The Solution: Show-and-Tell Plus Full Access

So we're pivoting to a more **demonstration-heavy format** with guided practice rather than free exploration. Think of it as:
- **70% us showing** our Russian military insights and HOW we got them
- **30% them trying** our methods on pre-loaded data subsets

The key difference from Fletcher: they'll be working with OUR Russian military data rather than finding their own research topics. This actually guarantees better outcomes in the time we have.

## The "Full Enchilada" Access

Here's the game-changer: Our new workshop app means participants get EVERYTHING afterward:
- Complete access to all 3 days of Fletcher materials
- Our Russian military research corpora (sanitized versions)
- The full tool pipeline with API keys
- All our visualization templates
- The "whole shebang" output system (corpus → middleware → 25-pager → ExSum → podcast → vodcast → NotebookLM)

So while the workshop itself is more "sending" than "receiving," they leave with the entire toolkit to go deep on their own time. It's like getting the full course materials after attending an intensive executive summary.

## The Strategic Value

For IRSEM, this format actually makes more sense:
1. They see proven results on exactly their area of interest (Russia)
2. They understand what's possible with these methods
3. They get hands-on practice with real data (not toy examples)
4. They leave with everything needed to implement

The compressed format forces us to lead with our strongest material - the actual Russian military analysis - rather than spending hours on bibliometric fundamentals.
